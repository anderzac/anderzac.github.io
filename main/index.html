EagleWorld Alpha 0.1.0.1

Gameplay:

EagleWorld is a grid-based video game written in Java for a high school project I did my senior year. There are 3 modes of play.

The first mode is a randomly created world. You can create various sizes of random worlds and they will fill randomly with various objects. The goal in this mode is to collect all of the money without being killed by a zombie.

The second mode is a custom created world. You can create any size you want bigger than 5x5. You can fill each coordinate in the world with an object of your choice one by one, or you can fill it randomly by clicking More>Fill.

The last mode is by playing pre-built levels. In this version there are only 2 levels. The goal in Zombie Apocalypse is to kill all of the zombies without dying. In Money Land, you just collect all the money. Simple enough

Things:

In EagleWorld Alpha, there are 8 things. Walls, the Player, Grass, Robots, Zombies, Sabers, Moneys, and Bunnies. Grass dies when stepped on and grows back into empty spaces near it. Robots run around in a circle. They mostly just get in the way. Zombies run to you and will kill you if you don't have a saber. Each saber can kill 3 zombies before it breaks and you need a new one. Each money has somewhere from $0 to $100 in it. Bunnies jump around to empty spots randomly. If they don't eat enough grass they will die.

Coming Soon:

EagleWorld Beta will have level loading. You can create and save worlds as levels and share them with others. There will also be a larger built in level pack.

Status Bar. Currently there is no way to know how much money or saber integrity you have left. The status bar may come with a life bar, so you don't die in one hit.
