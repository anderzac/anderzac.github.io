About Me

I grew up in the greater Seattle area and always had an aptitude and affinity for science, engineering, and art. In my high school years, I began developing those skills both in the classroom and at home. I loaded my schedule with AP classes and regularly used the vast resources available on the internet to do additional self-teaching. I found a love early on for technology, visual and media arts, and biology. My senior year, I took a robotics class and joined an after school robotics club, which was a fun introduction to engineering and got me interested in the computer side of engineering. My first programming language was Java, which I learned in an AP class at high school. At that point, I knew the direction I wanted to take my career.

From there I realized how much computer and techincal science there was to learn and also how little there was available in high school. I began a lot of self-teaching from this point even until now. I started learning web development, version control, microcontrollers, Linux, and much more through many resources scattered through the internet. I started school at Brigham Young University in Utah last fall and continued my education. I joined the computer engineering major, and took more computer science and engineering classes. This is when I started C++ and computer logic, including Verilog hardware description language (HDL).

Now I am back home in the Pacific Northwest looking for technical work in the area to continue my journey! I will be going back to school, but until at least fall of 2015, I will be here working.
